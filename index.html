The rule (checked weekly). CASH if NFCI’s 5-year percentile > 0.80 (held 3 months once triggered); else MODERATE (75% EQQQ + 25% money-market) if the Nasdaq-100 is below a rising 200-day average; else AGGRESSIVE (100% LQQ, 2× net). Aggressive sleeve = LQQ (Amundi 2× Nasdaq, Paris, EUR — carries EUR/USD exposure); Moderate = EQQQ; Cash = a GBP money-market fund. Backtest uses a synthetic 2×/3× Nasdaq-100 (daily leverage less financing + fees, validated against TQQQ at 0.999) and a money-market proxy; 7bps per switch.

Honest caveats. Max drawdown is −49% (2×) / −55% (3×) — this is leverage, it hurts. The edge over a plain weekly 200-day rule is real but rests on ~4 crash events in 26 years, and NFCI carries a ~1-week reporting lag. A personal research tracker, not investment advice.
